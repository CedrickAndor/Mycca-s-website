What is Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a kind of malware, or harmful software, that
replicates itself when it is run by altering and adding code to other programs on the computer. The virus can then infect more applications
or systems through the infected files or programs. Although viruses
are a particular kind of malware, the term "virus" is frequently used
to refer to several kinds of malware.

Key characteristics of computer viruses include:

1. Self-Replication

Viruses propagate to other programs or systems by replication. They attach themselves
to papers or executable files in order to accomplish this.


2. Destructive Intent

Numerous viruses contain dangerous code or a payload that can damage a computer system.
This could entail erasing or corrupting files, interfering with system functions, or pilfering confidential data.


3. Concealment

Viruses frequently try to conceal themselves in order to evade antivirus software's detection. They could get around security measures in a number of ways.


4. Actiation Trigger

Most viruses have an activation trigger, which could be a date or a set of circumstances. The virus starts its destructive work as soon as it is activated.


5. Host Dependence

For their propagation, viruses need host files or applications. They affix themselves to
authentic files, and the virus is active when those files are run.


6. Infection Vectors

Viruses can propagate via a number of channels, such as rogue websites, contaminated email attachments, removable storage devices, and network weaknesses.

Using antivirus software, updating operating systems and software,
being cautious when opening email attachments and accessing websites, and maintaining good cybersecurity hygiene are all part of protecting against computer viruses. Maintaining regular backups of crucial information significantly lessens the damage caused by malware attacks.

Examples of Computer Viruses

Computer viruses can be of many different kinds, and they are frequently categorized according to their traits, actions, and modes of operation. The following list includes typical computer virus types:

1. Boot Sector Viruses

  • characteristics: When a system is booted, these viruses can propagate via infecting storage devices' master boot records, or MBRs.
  • Example: Stoned, Michelangelo.

2. Worms

  • characteristics: Worms are self-replicating programs that propagate on their own, frequently via email or networks.
  • Example: Conficker, ILOVEYOU.

3. Trojan Horses

  • characteristics: Trojan horses pose as trustworthy software, but within they have malicious code capable of a wide range of destructive operations.
  • Example: Back Orifice, SubSeven.

4. Ransomware

  • characteristics: Ransomware encrypts a user's files and requests payment for the decryption key, typically in bitcoin.
  • Example: WannaCry, CryptoLocker

5. Adware and Spyware

  • characteristics: Spyware surreptitiously gathers data about user activity, whereas adware shows intrusive adverts.
  • Example: CoolWebSearch, Zango

These categories show the various methods that viruses utilize to
hack and infect computer systems. To defend against these attacks,
users must employ antivirus software, maintain good cybersecurity hygiene, and be watchful.

How to Avoid Computer Viruses

Avoiding computer viruses involves adopting good cybersecurity
practices to reduce the risk of infection. Aside from our previous
lessons here are some more tips to avoid malfunctions of our devices

1. Download from Trusted Sources

  • Download files, programs, and software only from reliable, official sources.
  • Software that has been cracked or pirated should not be downloaded as it can contain malware.

2. Be Careful with Removable Media

  • Before utilizing USB devices, external hard drives, and other removable media on your computer, be sure they are virus-free.
  • Turn off autorun to stop programs from removable media from running automatically.

3. Practice Safe Browsing

  • Steer clear of dubious or unreliable websites.
  • For safe and secure internet browsing, make use of HTTPS connections.

4. Educate Yourself

  • Keep yourself updated about typical social engineering and phishing tactics.
  • When disclosing personal information online, exercise caution.

5. Backup Your Data

  • Make sure you frequently backup your critical data to a secure online service or an external device.
  • You can use the backups to recover your data in the event of a ransomware or virus attack.

It's crucial to understand that although viruses are one
kind of malware, the word "malware" refers to a more
general class of malicious software that includes trojan
horses, worms, ransomware, spyware, and other
dangerous applications.
